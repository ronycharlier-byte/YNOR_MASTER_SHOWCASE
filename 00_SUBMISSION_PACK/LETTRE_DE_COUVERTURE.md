# COVER LETTER

Dear Editor,

Please consider our manuscript, "A Minimal Structural Ontology for Quantum Reality," for publication in Foundations of Physics.

The paper proposes a minimal structural ontological framework for quantum mechanics. Its central claim is that the quantum real is best understood not as a collection of substances with intrinsic properties, nor as entities embedded in a pre-given spacetime, but as a structure of constraints and contextual correlations from which events, causality, time, space, objects, identity, and information emerge as derived features. To articulate this proposal, the manuscript combines an ontological framework, a formal sketch grounded in the standard formalism of quantum mechanics, and a methodological principle (\(\mu\)) that selects ontologically admissible components by balancing explanatory power against ontological cost and interpretative friction.

The manuscript is designed to speak both to philosophy of physics and to the foundations of quantum mechanics. It does not advance a new physical theory, but rather offers a minimal structural ontology compatible with the standard quantum formalism, including Hilbert-space structure, POVMs, non-commutativity, entanglement, decoherence, and emergent spatiotemporal relations. The current version distinguishes formal claims, internal validation, and claims that still require external review or independent reproduction. We also include a comparative discussion positioning the proposal relative to Everettian, Bohmian, QBist, and relational approaches.

We believe the manuscript is well suited to Foundations of Physics because it directly addresses the conceptual structure and foundational interpretation of quantum theory at a high professional level. The paper is written as a perspective-style contribution with a formal core, and it clearly marks which statements are established by formal development and which remain to be externally validated. We hope it may be of interest to readers working on quantum foundations, structural realism, and the philosophy of quantum mechanics.
